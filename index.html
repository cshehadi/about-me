Charlie Shehadi

Introduction

I am web developer at Gotham Writers Workshop. I've been working there on and off since 1993. I currently live in Brooklyn, NY and I've got two daughters, ages 6 and 10. Here's my web site: Charlie's web site

As for what's important to me? Learning, growing, treating people decently, and living on a sane, safe and stable planet.

Where I'm From

I grew up in Princeton, New Jersey, in a family of 3 kids. My father was a philosophy professor and amateur singer of art songs, my mother, a high-school math teacher. We travelled quite a bit. I spent pre-school in London and Rome, 6th grade in Paris.

More About Me

What are your favorite hobbies?

Listening to music, eating, following the Minnesota Vikings, and playing with my kids. I'm also learning to play the piano.

What's your dream job?

Since I'm typing this at work, and someone could be looking over my shoulder, it's my current job, of course: Director of Technology at Gotham Writers Workshop.

What's your background?

A B.A. in philosophy from Vassar, an M.A. from the School of Visual Arts in Computer Art. 20 years of back-end web development.

What music have you been listening to lately?

Normally my tastes are pretty eclectic. I listen to classical (Beethoven, Brahms), jazz (Charlie Parker, Bill Frisell, John Scofield), and more recent music like RadioHead, The Strokes, and Spoon. Also, when I was in high school I was a huge prog rock fan, so I have to admit to still having a soft spot for that stuff. Bloated and silly as it was, I still get a kick out of it.

My eldest daughter just discovered Rock and Roll. I've been trying to remember what I liked at her age, so I can introduce her to it. So lately, I've been listening to Electric Light Orchestra, my favorite band when I was 10. It's a fun trip down memory lane, listening to music I probably haven't listened to for about 35 years. It's amazing how much of it I remember. That stuff you love when you're young gets burned into your soul - so you still love it, even if you end up seeing it as complete cheeseball music when you're an adult.
